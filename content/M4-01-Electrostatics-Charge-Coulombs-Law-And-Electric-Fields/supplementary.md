# Supplementary Materials

The read-along reference for electric charge, Coulomb's law and the electric field. Nothing here is spoken in the audio. Symbols: q, Q = charge (C); F = force (N); r = separation (m); k = Coulomb constant; E = electric field strength; V = voltage (V); d = plate separation (m).

### Listing 1 — Charge: the basics, conservation, quantisation
```text
TWO KINDS of charge: POSITIVE (protons) and NEGATIVE (electrons). Neutrons: no charge.
   RULE:  LIKE charges REPEL · UNLIKE charges ATTRACT.

NEUTRAL atom: equal protons & electrons. CHARGING moves ELECTRONS (light, outer),
   NOT protons (locked in nucleus).
   gain electrons → NEGATIVE ;  lose electrons → POSITIVE.

CONSERVATION of charge: charge cannot be created or destroyed, only TRANSFERRED.
   (one object −, another equally + ; total unchanged.)

QUANTISATION of charge: charge comes only in whole-number multiples of the
   ELEMENTARY CHARGE e ≈ 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C (charge of one electron/proton).
   unit = COULOMB (C); 1 C ≈ 6 × 10¹⁸ electrons (big — static is usually µC).
```

### Listing 2 — Three ways to charge an object
```text
FRICTION   rub two different INSULATORS → electrons transfer → one +, one −.
           (balloon on hair; shock after walking on carpet.)

CONDUCTION charged object TOUCHES a neutral conductor → charge shared on CONTACT.
(contact)  → conductor ends up with the SAME SIGN as the charging object.

INDUCTION  charged object brought NEAR (NO contact):
           1. its field SEPARATES the conductor's charges (near side opposite, far side same)
           2. EARTH the conductor (charge flows to/from ground)
           3. remove the earth, THEN remove the charging object
           → conductor ends up with the OPPOSITE SIGN to the charging object.

KEY CONTRAST:  CONDUCTION = contact, SAME sign  |  INDUCTION = no contact, OPPOSITE sign.
```

### Listing 3 — Coulomb's law (worked) & uniform field
```text
COULOMB'S LAW:   F = k q₁ q₂ / r²       k ≈ 9.0 × 10⁹ N m² C⁻²
   F ∝ product of charges (direct) ;  F ∝ 1/r² (inverse square, like gravity).
   same sign → REPULSIVE ; opposite sign → ATTRACTIVE.

PROPORTIONAL REASONING:
   double one charge → ×2 force ;  triple one charge → ×3 force
   double the distance → ÷4 force ;  halve the distance → ×4 force
   (double a charge AND double the distance → ×2 then ÷4 = ×½)

WORKED: q₁ = q₂ = 2×10⁻⁵ C, r = 0.3 m:
   F = (9×10⁹ × 2×10⁻⁵ × 2×10⁻⁵) / 0.3²
     = (9×10⁹ × 4×10⁻¹⁰) / 0.09 = 3.6 / 0.09 ≈ 40 N  (repulsive — both +)

UNIFORM FIELD (parallel plates):  E = V / d
   WORKED (1): V = 100 V, d = 0.02 m → E = 100/0.02 = 5000 V m⁻¹ (= N C⁻¹)
   WORKED (2): V = 200 V, d = 0.05 m → E = 4000 N C⁻¹ ;
               force on q = 3×10⁻⁶ C:  F = Eq = 4000 × 3×10⁻⁶ = 0.012 N
```

### Listing 4 — The electric field & field lines
```text
ELECTRIC FIELD STRENGTH:  E = F / q   (force per unit POSITIVE charge)
   unit: N C⁻¹  =  V m⁻¹ (equivalent).   force on a charge:  F = E q.
   FIELD = property of the LOCATION (exists with or without a charge there);
   FORCE = what a SPECIFIC charge q feels when placed in the field.
   E is a VECTOR: points the way a + test charge is pushed
   → AWAY from +, TOWARD −.

FIELD-LINE RULES:
   • lines start on + and end on −  (OUT of positive, INTO negative)
   • closer lines = stronger field
   • lines NEVER cross (field has one direction per point)

PATTERNS:
   point + charge : lines RADIATE OUTWARD (like an urchin)
   point − charge : lines point RADIALLY INWARD
   dipole (+ and −): lines curve from + to −
   parallel plates : straight, parallel, EVENLY SPACED, + plate → − plate
                     = UNIFORM field (same E everywhere; E = V/d).
                     → sets up M6: charged particle accelerated/deflected.
```
