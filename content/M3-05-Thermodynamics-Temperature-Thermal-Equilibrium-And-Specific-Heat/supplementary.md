# Supplementary Materials

The read-along reference for the foundations of thermodynamics: temperature, heat, thermal equilibrium and specific heat capacity. Nothing here is spoken in the audio. Symbols: Q = heat energy (J); m = mass (kg); c = specific heat capacity (J kg⁻¹ K⁻¹); ΔT = temperature change (K or °C).

### Listing 1 — Temperature, heat and internal energy
```text
KINETIC PARTICLE MODEL: all matter is made of particles in constant motion
   (solids vibrate in place · liquids move while close · gases fly about freely),
   so the particles have KINETIC ENERGY.

TEMPERATURE  = a measure of the AVERAGE kinetic energy of the particles.
               hotter → particles faster on average.  (it is an AVERAGE of a SPREAD
               of particle speeds — basis of evaporation & of black-body radiation, M7-10.)

HEAT (thermal energy transfer) = energy that FLOWS from a HOTTER to a COLDER object
               because of the temperature difference.  "energy IN TRANSIT."

INTERNAL ENERGY = the TOTAL kinetic + potential energy of ALL an object's particles.
   ⇒ an object does NOT "contain heat"; it contains internal energy.
   ⇒ HIGH temperature ≠ lots of energy:  a spark (tiny mass, few particles) is hotter
     than a warm bath but holds far LESS internal energy.

THERMAL EQUILIBRIUM: two objects in contact reach the SAME temperature → NO net heat
   flow.  Heat always flows HOT → COLD (never the reverse on its own).
   Zeroth law: A eq. with B, B eq. with C ⇒ A and C same temperature (makes thermometers work).
```

### Listing 2 — Temperature scales
```text
CELSIUS (°C):  0 °C = water freezes, 100 °C = water boils. Zero is ARBITRARY.

KELVIN (K): the ABSOLUTE scale. 0 K = ABSOLUTE ZERO = minimum particle motion
            = −273 °C (more precisely −273.15 °C).
   Same degree SIZE as Celsius (ΔT of 1 K = ΔT of 1 °C).

CONVERSION:    K = °C + 273
   0 °C = 273 K (ice melts) · 100 °C = 373 K (water boils) · 25 °C = 298 K · −273 °C = 0 K

WHY PHYSICS USES KELVIN: Kelvin temperature is DIRECTLY PROPORTIONAL to the average
   kinetic energy of the particles (double the K → double the average KE).
   Celsius is NOT, because its zero is in the "wrong" place.
```

### Listing 3 — Specific heat capacity: ΔQ = mcΔT
```text
SPECIFIC HEAT CAPACITY c = energy to raise 1 kg of a substance by 1 K (J kg⁻¹ K⁻¹).
   high c → hard to heat/cool (needs lots of energy) ; low c → heats & cools easily.

   EQUATION:   ΔQ = m c ΔT       (ΔT is a CHANGE, not an absolute temperature!)
   rearranged: ΔT = Q / (m c)     c = Q / (m ΔT)     m = Q / (c ΔT)

WATER: c ≈ 4200 J kg⁻¹ K⁻¹ — unusually HIGH (metals are only a few hundred).
   ⇒ good COOLANT (engines, power stations); OCEANS moderate coastal climate;
     pot of water slow to boil while the metal pot heats instantly.

WORKED EXAMPLES:
   (1) Heat 2 kg water by 30 K:  Q = 2 × 4200 × 30 = 252 000 J = 252 kJ
   (2) Heat 0.5 kg water 20 °C → 80 °C (ΔT = 60):  Q = 0.5 × 4200 × 60 = 126 000 J = 126 kJ
   (3) Reverse — 63 000 J into 0.5 kg copper (c = 390):
           ΔT = Q/(mc) = 63 000 / (0.5 × 390) = 63 000 / 195 ≈ 323 K (huge rise — low c!)

MIXING (insulated): energy conserved → HEAT LOST by hot = HEAT GAINED by cold
   m_hot c_hot ΔT_hot = m_cold c_cold ΔT_cold  → solve for the common final temperature.
   The substance with the SMALLER m·c changes temperature MORE.
```
