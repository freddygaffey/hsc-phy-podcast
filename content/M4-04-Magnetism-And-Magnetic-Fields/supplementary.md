# Supplementary Materials

The read-along reference for magnetic poles, materials, and the fields of magnets, wires and solenoids. Nothing here is spoken in the audio. Symbols: B = magnetic field strength (T, tesla); I = current (A); r = distance from wire (m); N = number of turns; L = length (m); μ₀ = permeability of free space = 4π × 10⁻⁷.

### Listing 1 — Poles and magnetic materials
```text
POLES: every magnet has a NORTH and a SOUTH pole.
   RULE:  like poles REPEL · unlike poles ATTRACT.
   NO MONOPOLES: poles ALWAYS come in pairs. Cut a magnet → two smaller COMPLETE magnets
      (each with its own N and S), however finely you divide it.

FERROMAGNETIC materials (strongly magnetic): IRON, NICKEL, COBALT, + alloys (steel).
   most materials (wood, plastic, copper, aluminium) are NOT magnetic this way.

DOMAINS (how magnetisation works):
   • a domain = a tiny region acting as a small magnet (its atoms' fields aligned).
   • UNMAGNETISED: domains point RANDOMLY → fields cancel → no net magnetism.
   • MAGNETISED: in an external field, domains ALIGN → fields ADD → strong net magnetism.
   • heating/hammering jostles domains back to random → magnet can lose magnetism.

SOFT vs HARD:
   soft (pure iron): magnetises & DE-magnetises easily → electromagnet CORES (switchable).
   hard (steel): hard to magnetise but KEEPS it → PERMANENT magnets, compass needles.
```

### Listing 2 — Magnetic field lines & the Earth
```text
MAGNETIC FIELD = region where a magnetic force is felt; shown by FIELD LINES.
   • outside a magnet, lines run NORTH → SOUTH (direction a compass N-pole points).
   • closer lines = stronger field ;  lines NEVER cross.
   • bar magnet: lines loop out of N, around, back into S (closed loops).
   • a compass needle aligns ALONG the field (its N-seeking end points the field's way).

EARTH = behaves like a giant BAR MAGNET.
   ⚠ geographic NORTH pole is a magnetic SOUTH (it ATTRACTS a compass's north end;
     unlike poles attract).  Field guides compasses & deflects solar particles → auroras.

ELECTROMAGNET = SOLENOID + ferromagnetic (iron) CORE.
   core's domains align & add to the coil's field → far stronger (×hundreds).
   ADVANTAGES over permanent magnet: switch ON/OFF (only magnetic while current flows);
      vary strength (change current or turns); reverse poles (reverse current).
   → basis of motors, generators, transformers, loudspeakers, maglev (Module 6).
```

### Listing 3 — Fields of wires and solenoids (with the right-hand rules)
```text
OERSTED (1820): a current deflects a compass → ELECTRICITY and MAGNETISM are LINKED.
   ⇒ a MOVING CHARGE (current) PRODUCES a magnetic field.

LONG STRAIGHT WIRE:
   field = CONCENTRIC CIRCLES centred on the wire (⊥ to it); strength falls with distance.
   B = μ₀ I / (2 π r)      (B ∝ I ;  B ∝ 1/r)
   DIRECTION — RIGHT-HAND GRIP RULE: thumb = conventional CURRENT, curled fingers = FIELD.
   WORKED: I = 10 A, r = 0.05 m:
       B = (4π×10⁻⁷ × 10) / (2π × 0.05) = 4×10⁻⁵ T (≈ 40 µT, ~Earth's field)

SOLENOID (coil of wire):
   field looks like a BAR MAGNET — N pole at one end, S at the other; uniform inside.
   B = μ₀ N I / L      (↑ by more current, more turns, or shorter length / closer turns)
   DIRECTION — RIGHT-HAND rule: fingers curl with the CURRENT around the coils,
       THUMB points to the NORTH end.
   WORKED: N = 200, I = 3 A, L = 0.5 m:
       B = (4π×10⁻⁷ × 200 × 3) / 0.5 ≈ 1.5×10⁻³ T (1.5 mT)

⚠ wire field = CIRCLES around it ; solenoid field = BAR-MAGNET pattern with poles. Don't mix.
```
