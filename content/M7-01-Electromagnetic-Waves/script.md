NARRATOR: Fourth question, and it's the conceptual heart of the whole module: no medium. Here's the historical setup, and it matters because it threads through the back half of Module 7. NARRATOR: One subtlety to keep correct, because it trips people up. When light goes through a transparent material, glass, water, air, it travels slower than it does in a vacuum. NARRATOR: Which brings us to the fifth and biggest question: why is the speed constant, and why is it the same one speed for every kind of electromagnetic wave? Think back to the propagation loop.
