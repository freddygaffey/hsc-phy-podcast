# Supplementary Materials

Energy equations and worked solutions. Nothing here is spoken in the audio — it is the read-along reference. Symbols: W = work; F = force; s = displacement; m = mass; v = speed; g ≈ 9.8 m s⁻²; Δh = change in height; P = power; E = energy.

### Listing 1 — Work, energy and power equations
```text
WORK:               W = F s   (force × displacement IN the direction of the force); scalar, joules (J)
                    No work if: no displacement, OR force ⟂ to motion.
KINETIC ENERGY:     KE = ½ m v²   (J)
WORK–ENERGY THEOREM: net work = change in kinetic energy   (W_net = ΔKE)
GRAV. POTENTIAL:    ΔU = m g Δh   (depends ONLY on vertical height change)
CONSERVATION (no friction): KE + U = constant  ⇒  PE lost = KE gained:  m g Δh = ½ m v²
POWER:              P = ΔE / t   (rate of energy transfer; watts, W = J s⁻¹)
                    P = F v       (force × velocity — useful at constant speed)

⚠ Energy is TRANSFORMED, never "lost"/"used up"/"destroyed" (conservation of energy).
```

### Listing 2 — Worked examples: conservation of energy
```text
PE lost = KE gained:  m g Δh = ½ m v²  ⇒  v = √(2 g Δh)   (mass cancels!)

(A) Ball dropped 5 m:  v = √(2 × 9.8 × 5) = √98 ≈ 9.9 m s⁻¹
(B) Q2: object falls 10 m:  v = √(2 × 9.8 × 10) = √196 = 14 m s⁻¹
(C) Q5: trolley 6 m s⁻¹ up a frictionless ramp, max height:
       ½ m v² = m g Δh  ⇒  Δh = v²/(2g) = 36/19.6 ≈ 1.84 m
       (rough ramp: friction → heat, so less KE becomes PE → lower height)
(D) Work–energy theorem: F_net = 100 N over 20 m, from rest, m = 10 kg:
       W_net = 100 × 20 = 2000 J = ½mv²  ⇒  v² = 400  ⇒  v = 20 m s⁻¹
```

### Listing 3 — Worked examples: work and power
```text
(A) Crane lifts 500 kg to 20 m in 10 s (Q3):
       W = m g Δh = 500 × 9.8 × 20 = 98 000 J
       P = W / t = 98 000 / 10 = 9800 W = 9.8 kW
(B) Car at constant 28 m s⁻¹ against 700 N resistance (P = Fv):
       constant speed ⇒ driving force = resistance = 700 N
       P = F v = 700 × 28 ≈ 19 600 W ≈ 20 kW
```

### Listing 4 — Forms of energy and conservation
```text
ENERGY = the capacity to do work; a SCALAR, in joules.

Forms: kinetic · gravitational potential · elastic potential · thermal (heat) ·
       chemical · light · sound · electrical · nuclear.

LAW OF CONSERVATION OF ENERGY: the total energy of an isolated system is constant —
   energy is transformed between forms, never created or destroyed.

Trampolinist: chemical → kinetic → gravitational PE → kinetic → elastic PE → kinetic …
   (a little → heat + sound each bounce, so they slow without re-pushing).

Work done AGAINST a resistance (friction/air/rolling) = resistive force × distance
   = mechanical energy transformed to HEAT.  "Transformed, not lost."
```
