### Listing 2 — Radioactivity: the three decays and the bookkeeping
| Property | Alpha (α) | Beta (β⁻) | Gamma (γ) |
|----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Nature | Helium-4 nucleus | Fast electron | High-energy photon |
| Charge | +2 | −1 | 0 |
| Ionising power | Highest | Medium | Lowest |
| Penetration | Lowest (paper) | Medium (mm Al) | Highest (cm Pb) |

Inverse rule: high ionising ⇒ low penetration (and vice versa).

**Decay equations** (conserve mass number \(A\), atomic number \(Z\), and mass-energy):

- Alpha: \(A \to A-4\), \(Z \to Z-2\) (emits \({}^{4}_{2}\mathrm{He}\))
- Beta⁻: \(A\) same, \(Z \to Z+1\); \(n \to p + e^{-} + \bar{\nu}\) (quark: \(d \to u\), weak force / W)
- Beta⁺: \(A\) same, \(Z \to Z-1\); \(p \to n + e^{+} + \nu\) (quark: \(u \to d\), weak force / W)
- Gamma: \(A\) same, \(Z\) same; excited nucleus sheds a photon

NEUTRINO: beta particles emerge with a RANGE of energies ⇒ Pauli predicted the neutrino to carry the missing energy & momentum (same logic Chadwick used → neutron).

**Half-life:**

$$
N = N_0\, e^{-\lambda t}, \qquad \lambda = \frac{\ln 2}{t_{1/2}} \approx \frac{0.693}{t_{1/2}}
$$

short \(t_{1/2}\) ⇒ large \(\lambda\) ⇒ fast decay. Match \(t_{1/2}\) + radiation type to the job.

### Listing 3 — The Standard Model (matter particles and forces)

**Quarks (6):** up, down, charm, strange, top, bottom. Charges: up-type \(= +2/3\); down-type \(= -1/3\).

$$
\text{Proton} = u\,u\,d \to +\tfrac{2}{3} + \tfrac{2}{3} - \tfrac{1}{3} = +1
$$

$$
\text{Neutron} = u\,d\,d \to +\tfrac{2}{3} - \tfrac{1}{3} - \tfrac{1}{3} = 0
$$

(say the charge sum out loud — easy mark)

**Leptons (6):** electron, muon, tau (each charge \(-1\)) + their three neutrinos. Everyday matter = first generation only: up quark, down quark, electron.

| Force | Relative strength | Carrier boson |
|-------|-------------------|---------------|
| Strong | strongest | gluon |
| Electromagnetic | — | photon |
| Weak | — | W and Z bosons |
| Gravity | weakest | **none in the Standard Model** (flag this) |

### Listing 5 — The binding-energy-per-nucleon curve (landmarks)

Plot: binding energy PER NUCLEON \((y)\) vs mass number \(A\) \((x)\).

```text
   BE/nucleon
   (MeV)        ___ iron-56 ≈ 8.8  ← PEAK = most tightly bound, most stable nucleus
      9 |      /‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾———____
        |     /  ⁴He≈7.07          ‾‾‾‾———____ U-235
        |    /                                    (heavy, less bound)
        |  /²H
      0 |_/________________________________________→  mass number A
         light                 56                 heavy
```

KEY RULE: a reaction releases energy when its products move HIGHER on the curve (toward iron) → more tightly bound → mass defect released as \(E = mc^{2}\).

- FUSION: light nuclei (left) combine → climb toward iron → energy released
- FISSION: heavy nuclei (right) split → climb toward iron → energy released
- IRON LIMIT: iron-56 is the peak — nothing higher to climb to, so fusing iron or beyond ABSORBS energy. ⇒ stellar fusion stops at iron; heavier elements need supernovae.

USEFUL NUMBERS: \(1\ \mathrm{u} \leftrightarrow 931.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (BE in MeV = mass defect in u \(\times\ 931.5\)). \({}^{4}\mathrm{He}\) total BE \(\approx 28.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (7.07/nucleon); iron-56 \(\approx 8.8\)/nucleon; U-235 fission \(\approx 200\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

### Listing 6 — Atomic-model development skeleton (evidence → killer → conclusion)

Each model was BUILT by evidence and KILLED by one observation it couldn't explain. ("Can Plum Give Birth, Wonderful" — Cathode, Plum, Gold, Bohr, Waves)

**1. Cathode rays → the electron (J.J. Thomson)**

- Maltese-cross shadow → travel in straight lines
- paddle wheel turns → carry momentum / mass
- bend to + plate (E) → negative charge
- deflect in B field → charged particles, not light

⇒ electron: negative sub-atomic particle. Thomson measured charge-to-mass \((q/m \approx 1.76 \times 10^{11}\ \mathrm{C/kg})\); Millikan (oil drop) measured the charge alone \((e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\ \mathrm{C})\), showing charge is quantised.

**2. Plum pudding (Thomson):** electrons in a sphere of diffuse positive charge. KILLED BY → the gold-foil experiment.

**3. Nuclear atom (Rutherford; Geiger–Marsden gold foil)**

- most α pass through → atom mostly empty space
- few α deflected greatly → positive charge concentrated
- ~1 in 8000 rebound → tiny, dense, massive nucleus (α REPELLED, like charges)

KILLED BY → Maxwell: an orbiting (accelerating) electron must radiate, spiral in \((< 10^{-8}\ \mathrm{s})\) and give a CONTINUOUS spectrum — but atoms are stable with DISCRETE lines.

**4. Bohr:** 3 postulates — stationary states (no radiation); photon on a jump \((hf = E_{\text{high}} - E_{\text{low}}\), use the MAGNITUDE\()\); angular momentum quantised. Reproduces hydrogen lines; \(E_n = -13.6/n^{2}\ \mathrm{eV}\). LIMITS → works only for hydrogen / one-electron ions; can't explain line intensities or Zeeman splitting; never explains WHY orbits are quantised.

**5. Matter waves** (de Broglie \(\lambda = h/p\); Davisson–Germer & G.P. Thomson electron diffraction) ⇒ only orbits whose circumference = a whole number of wavelengths form a standing wave → THIS is why orbits are quantised (the mechanism Bohr only asserted). (Marks are in the STANDING-WAVE argument, not in quoting \(\lambda = h/p\).) Then Schrödinger (orbitals/probability) + Heisenberg (uncertainty).

UNDERNEATH: nucleons are made of quarks (Standard Model, Listing 3).
