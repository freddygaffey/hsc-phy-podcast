# Supplementary Materials

The read-along reference for wave properties and the wave equation. Nothing here is spoken in the audio. Symbols: v = wave speed; f = frequency; λ = wavelength; T = period; A = amplitude.

### Listing 1 — Wave quantities and the wave equation
```text
WHAT A WAVE IS:  a disturbance that transfers ENERGY through a medium (or space)
                 WITHOUT a net transfer of MATTER. Particles oscillate about a
                 fixed rest position; the energy/pattern travels, the matter does not.

QUANTITY      SYMBOL   MEANING                                   UNIT
  wavelength    λ      distance for ONE complete cycle           m      (crest→crest)
  amplitude     A      max displacement from rest position       m      (energy ∝ A²)
  period        T      time for ONE complete cycle               s
  frequency     f      cycles per second                         Hz     (= 1/T)
  wave speed    v      speed the disturbance/energy travels      m s⁻¹

KEY RELATIONS:   f = 1 / T          (frequency and period are reciprocals)
                 v = f λ            THE WAVE EQUATION
                 rearranged:  f = v / λ      λ = v / f

WHY v = fλ:  in one period T the wave advances exactly one wavelength λ,
             so v = λ / T = λ × (1/T) = f λ.

WAVE SPEED is set by the MEDIUM, not the source. Shake the source faster (↑f) and
λ shortens to keep v fixed ⇒ f and λ are INVERSELY related at constant speed.
```

### Listing 2 — Worked v = fλ examples
```text
(1) f = 50 Hz, λ = 2 m:           v = fλ = 50 × 2 = 100 m s⁻¹
    if f doubles & v fixed:        λ halves to 1 m  (v = fλ, inverse relation)

(2) Water wave: λ = 3 m, f = 0.5 Hz:
        v = fλ = 0.5 × 3 = 1.5 m s⁻¹
        T = 1/f = 1/0.5 = 2 s

(3) Two-graph combine: T = 0.4 s (from displacement–time graph),
                       λ = 0.8 m (from displacement–position snapshot):
        f = 1/T = 1/0.4 = 2.5 Hz
        v = fλ = 2.5 × 0.8 = 2 m s⁻¹

(4) Sound, air→water (frequency FIXED by source):
        air:   v = 340 m s⁻¹, f = 680 Hz → λ = v/f = 340/680 = 0.5 m
        water: v ≈ 1500 m s⁻¹, f = 680 Hz → λ = v/f = 1500/680 ≈ 2.2 m
        f unchanged across the boundary; v↑ ⇒ λ↑ (0.5 m → ~2.2 m).
        (This is refraction — returns in the light episodes.)

(5) f from period off a graph: 4 cycles in 1.2 s → T = 1.2/4 = 0.3 s → f = 1/0.3 ≈ 3.3 Hz
```

### Listing 3 — The two classifications
```text
TRANSVERSE vs LONGITUDINAL  (by particle motion vs direction of travel)
  TRANSVERSE    particles oscillate PERPENDICULAR to travel.
                e.g. wave on a string; ALL electromagnetic waves (light).
  LONGITUDINAL  particles oscillate PARALLEL to travel; makes COMPRESSIONS
                (bunched) and RAREFACTIONS (spread out).  e.g. SOUND in air.

MECHANICAL vs ELECTROMAGNETIC  (by whether a medium is needed)
  MECHANICAL        needs a medium (vibrates its particles) — CANNOT cross a vacuum.
                    e.g. sound, water waves, waves on a string. (no sound in space)
  ELECTROMAGNETIC   oscillating electric & magnetic fields — needs NO medium,
                    travels through vacuum at the speed of light (3×10⁸ m s⁻¹).
                    e.g. light, radio, X-rays.

   ┌───────────┬───────────────┬───────────────────┐
   │           │ MECHANICAL    │ ELECTROMAGNETIC   │
   ├───────────┼───────────────┼───────────────────┤
   │ SOUND     │ ✓ longitudinal│                   │
   │ LIGHT     │               │ ✓ transverse      │
   └───────────┴───────────────┴───────────────────┘
   ⇒ "see the explosion before you hear it": light (EM, fast) beats sound (mechanical, slow).

EM SPECTRUM (↑frequency, ↓wavelength):  Radio · Microwave · Infrared · Visible ·
   Ultraviolet · X-ray · Gamma   — "Raging Martians Invaded Venus Using X-ray Guns"
   All travel at the SAME speed (c) in vacuum; differ only in f and λ (v = fλ).
```

### Listing 4 — Reading the two wave graphs
```text
CHECK THE HORIZONTAL AXIS FIRST — the two graphs look alike but mean different things.

  DISPLACEMENT vs TIME      (one particle, over time)
      → gives the PERIOD T (one cycle on the time axis), hence f = 1/T.
      → does NOT give wavelength.

  DISPLACEMENT vs POSITION  ("snapshot": all particles, one instant)
      → gives the WAVELENGTH λ (one cycle on the distance axis).
      → does NOT give period/frequency.

  BOTH graphs show AMPLITUDE (height from the centre line to a crest).

READING TIPS:
  • Measure across MANY whole cycles and divide (4 cycles in 1.2 s ⇒ T = 0.3 s).
  • Amplitude is centre→crest, NOT trough→crest (that is 2A).
  • One wavelength = crest→crest = trough→trough = any repeat point to the next.
  • To get speed you usually need BOTH graphs: f from the time graph, λ from the
    position graph, then v = fλ.

COMMON TRAPS (each a mark):
  1. "a wave transfers matter" — NO, it transfers energy; particles only oscillate.
  2. transverse ↔ longitudinal mix-up — perpendicular = transverse, parallel = longitudinal.
  3. "all waves need a medium" — EM waves do not.
  4. reading λ off a displacement–time graph — that gives T, not λ.
  5. amplitude ≠ wavelength — amplitude is height, wavelength is length of a cycle.
```
